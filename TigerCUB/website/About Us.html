The team behind TigerCUB

Who we are, what we've done, what we hope for

We built this website during HackPrinceton 2014, with help and support from Mongo DB, Microsoft, GitHub, Django, VirtualENV, and Bootstrap.

TigerCUB, short for Tiger Certificate Utility for Bachelors, is a website that will help Princeton students find certificates. Based on courses that students have taken in the past, TigerCUB's powerful engine analyzes Princeton's course database and finds the most interesting and most convenient certificates for students to obtain. It will then provide a sample course schedule that will allow students to complete these certificate requirements.

The difficulties with trying to find out the current requirements for certificates are immense. Not only do students have to navigate a variety of different websites with completely different UIs, they may even miss out on certificates that could really appeal to them. TigerCUB can show certificates that work with students' interests and skills, and can inspire Princetonians to take their learning in new directions.

Akash Levy, Bharath Srivatsan, Mohamed El-Dirany, and Yash Huilgol are freshmen at Princeton University.
